Must-Visit Places

A giant torii gate in front of the Romon Gate at the shrine's entrance

Fushimi Inari Shrine

Fushimi Inari Shrine (伏見稲荷大社, Fushimi Inari Taisha) is an important Shinto shrine in southern Kyoto, also famous for its numerous Torii Gates.

Learn More
Temple building covered in gold

Kinkakuji (Golden Pavilion)

Kinkakuji (金閣寺, Golden Pavilion) is a Zen temple in northern Kyoto whose top two floors are completely covered in gold leaf.

Learn More
Former Kyoto residence of the shogun

Nijo Castle (Nijojo)

Nijo Castle (二条城, Nijōjō) was built in 1603 as the Kyoto residence of Tokugawa Ieyasu, the first shogun of the Edo Period (1603-1868).

Learn More
Pleasant district at the outskirts of Kyoto

Arashiyama and Sagano

Arashiyama (嵐山) is a pleasant, touristy district in the western outskirts of Kyoto. The area has been a popular destination since the Heian Period (794-1185), when nobles would enjoy its natural setting.

Famous Kyoto Events

The famous Gion Matsuri festival in Kyoto

Gion Matsuri

The Gion Matsuri (祇園祭り) is one of Japan's largest and most famous festivals, held annually in Kyoto in July.

Kiyomizu-dera, autumn light-up event

Kiyomizu-dera Light Up

The Kiyomizu-dera Light Up event takes place in autumn, where the temple and surrounding scenery are beautifully illuminated.

Sakura at Kyoto Imperial Palace

Kyoto Imperial Palace Sakura Viewing

Kyoto Imperial Palace opens its gardens for cherry blossom viewing each spring, offering beautiful views of sakura flowers.

To-ji Temple Flea Market (Kōbō-san)

To-ji Temple Flea Market

To-ji Temple's monthly flea market, called Kōbō-san, is a great place to shop for antiques and local goods.
